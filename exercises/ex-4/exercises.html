The LGBTQ+ Community and What It Is

LGBTQ+ is an acronym composed of terms that are used to describe a person's sexual orientation or gender identity


  • Lesbian - A lesbian is a female homosexual: a female who experiences romantic love or sexual attraction to other females.

  • Gay - Gay is a term that primarily refers to a homosexual person or the trait of being homosexual. Gay is often used to describe homosexual males but lesbians may also be referred to as gay.

  • Bisexual - Bisexuality is romantic attraction, sexual attraction or sexual behavior toward both males and females, or romantic or sexual attraction to people of any sex or gender identity; this latter aspect is sometimes termed pansexuality.

  • Transgender - Transgender is an umbrella term for people whose gender identity differs from what is typically associated with the sex they were assigned at birth. It is sometimes abbreviated to trans.

  • Queer/Questioning - Queer is an umbrella term for sexual and gender minorities that are not heterosexual or cisgender. Queer was originally used pejoratively against those with same-sex desires but, beginning in the late-1980s, queer scholars and activists began to reclaim the word.

  • + - There are many more terms that have been added to the list over the years. These include: Intersex, Asexual, Ally, Pansexual, Agender, Gender Queer, Bigender, Gender Variant and Pangender
